# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Product Wiki community a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background or identity. We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and healthy community.

## Our standards

This project adopts the **Contributor Covenant, version 2.1** as its standard of behaviour. The full text, including the detailed examples of acceptable and unacceptable behaviour, is published at:

https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

In short: be respectful, assume good intent, give and accept constructive feedback gracefully, and keep criticism focused on the work rather than the person. Behaviour the Covenant lists as unacceptable (harassment, personal attacks, publishing others' private information, and other conduct that is inappropriate in a professional setting) is not tolerated here.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, and code) and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported privately to the maintainer at **hi@omarismail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and the reporter's privacy and security will be respected.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines (from Contributor Covenant 2.1) when determining the consequences of a violation:

1. **Correction**: a private, written warning for unprofessional or unwelcome behaviour, with clarity about why it was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning**: a warning with consequences for continued behaviour, including a defined period of no interaction with those involved.
3. **Temporary ban**: a temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a serious or sustained violation.
4. **Permanent ban**: a permanent ban from public interaction within the community for a pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression.
